## Meter Stick

- Divisions:
    - 1 m (entire meter stick)
    - 1/10 m (every 10 cm gets a larger number)
    - 1/100 m (every cm gets a number and a long tick mark)
    - 5/1000 m (every 5 mm gets a medium tick mark)
    - 1/1000 m (every mm gets a small tick mark)

## U.S. Customary Yardsticks and Tape Measures

- Divisions:
    - 1 yd = 36 in (entire yardstick)
    - 1/3 yd = 1 ft = 12 in (seen more often on tape measures than yardsticks)
    - 1/36 yd = 1/12 ft = 1 in (each inch is labeled with a large tick mark)
    - 1/72 yd = 1/24 ft = 1/2 in (next smaller tick marks)
    - 1/144 yd = 1/48 ft = 1/4 in (next smaller tick marks)
    - 1/288 yd = 1/96 ft = 1/8 in (next smaller tick marks)

## U.S. Customary Ruler

- Divisions
    - 1 ft = 12 in (entire ruler)
    - 1/12 ft = 1 in (inches are labeled and have the largest tick marks)
    - 1/24 ft = 1/2 in (next smaller tick marks)
    - 1/48 ft = 1/8 in (next smaller...)
    - 1/96 ft = 1/16 in (next smaller...)
    - 1/92 ft = 1/32 in

## Related Patterns

- Ignoring the length of the tick marks, a ruler is a set of [uniform stripes](../patterns/stripes.md)
- The tick marks represent fractions of the length of the ruler
    - For metric rulers, subdivisions use powers of 10
    - For U.S. customary inches, the subdivisions use powers of 2. In other words, they are [dyadic rationals (wiki)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dyadic_rational)
- If you continue subdividing rulers using the same scaling factor, you'll fill in the whole line (see [fractal ruler](../patterns/fractal-ruler.md))
